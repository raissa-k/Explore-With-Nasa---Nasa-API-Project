About this project

NASA has a comprehensive list of APIs that allow access to all sorts of information, and this website allows you to check some of it.

In the Picture of the Day section, a simple click will show you today's snippet of our universe. Despite the name of the section, a click may return a video, too!

If you're feeling adventurous you can hop into the Time Machine and retrieve a photo or video from the archive!

In fact, the Time Machine is PERFECT for killing those few minutes before clocking out, or for helping you craft a nice message with subtle "sorry I forgot to buy flowers" undertones.

Picture of the Day

Here's your daily dose of space goodness.

Each day a different image or photograph of our fascinating universe is featured, along with a brief explanation written by a professional astronomer.

In NASA's own words:

One of the most popular websites at NASA is the Astronomy Picture of the Day. In fact, this website is one of the most popular websites across all federal agencies. It has the popular appeal of a Justin Bieber video.

And you know what? I beliebe that.

Time machine

Curious about what the Astronomy Picture was on a specific day? Choose the date and find out!

Note that the first Astronomy Picture of the Day was posted on June 16th, 1995, so that's how far back you can go.
